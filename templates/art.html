Japanese art styles vary by genre

The Japanese art includes a wide range of styles and means of expression, including ceramics, sculpture, painting and calligraphy on silk and paper, the ukiyo-e woodblock prints, origami and, more recently, manga along with a myriad of other types of artwork.The first examples of complex art in Japan was produced in the centuries VII and VIII in connection with Buddhism. In the ninth century, when the Japanese began to free itself from the cultural influence of China and develop indigenous forms of expression, the secular arts became increasingly important. Until the end of the fifteenth century, both religious and secular art had flourished.Japanese had formed a brand new envisioning of the buddha and applied that into their work.
